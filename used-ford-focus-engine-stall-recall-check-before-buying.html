Ford Recalls 255,404 Focus Cars for Engine Stall (26V369): What Used-Focus Buyers Must Check

Ford is recalling 255,404 2012-2018 Focus cars (NHTSA recall 26V369, Ford 26S40) because a stuck-open canister purge valve can stall the engine while driving. These are cars whose 2018 recall repair was recorded complete but not correctly installed, or could not be verified. The fix is a free dealer software update; owner letters mail from July 6, 2026. A VIN check can confirm the recall's presence — only Ford or a dealer can confirm the fix.

Key facts

  • Recall numbers: NHTSA 26V369; Ford 26S40. Part 573 report filed June 9, 2026; posted by NHTSA June 12, 2026.
  • Vehicles: 255,404 Ford Focus, model years 2012-2018, built October 4, 2010 through December 20, 2017. Estimated defect rate: 100% of the recalled population.
  • Why these cars: they are a subset (~20%) of the 1,282,596 Focuses recalled in October 2018 (18V735/18S32) whose remedy software was either confirmed incorrect or could not be verified in Ford's records.
  • Defect: the canister purge valve (CPV) can stick open; during the evaporative leak monitor check this creates excessive vacuum that can deform the plastic fuel tank. The PCM software may not adequately detect the stuck valve. Result, in Ford's words: "An engine stall while driving without warning or without the ability to restart, can increase the risk of a crash."
  • Warning signs: check engine light (MIL), inaccurate fuel gauge, inaccurate distance-to-empty, drivability concerns.
  • Remedy: free powertrain control module (PCM) software update at a Ford dealer. Software only — not valve replacement, and not over-the-air.
  • Dates: dealer notification July 6-8, 2026; owner letters July 6-10, 2026; VINs searchable on NHTSA.gov from July 6, 2026.
  • Risk advisories: none — no "do not drive" and no "park outside" order. Ford reports no known accidents or injuries from this condition.
  • Contact: Ford customer service 1-866-436-7332.

Why is the 2012-2018 Ford Focus being recalled again?

Recall 26V369 is a re-recall, not a newly discovered defect. Per Ford's Part 573 filing, certain 2012-2018 Focus vehicles "did not have the remedy for Safety Recall 18S32 / 18V735 installed correctly, but were recorded as having the repair successfully completed." In other words, Ford's dealer records said these cars were fixed in the 2018-2019 purge-valve campaign when the corrective PCM software was never properly applied — or the records were too incomplete to prove it either way.

The chronology in the filing shows how it unraveled. Ford's Critical Concern Review Group took up the issue in November 2024 after a quality audit, informed NHTSA of software mismatches in December 2024, and a March 2025 audit of software recalls going back to 2017 found discrepancies tied to the transition from Ford's legacy IDS service tool to the newer FDRS tool — plus "a significant lack of historical data." In April 2026 the group determined several recalls may have been closed without the remedy installed; by May 27, 2026, an audit confirmed only a subset of completed 18S32 repairs had matching software. Ford approved the field action on June 2 and filed with NHTSA on June 9.

The recalled population includes two groups: VINs confirmed to have the wrong software, and VINs whose recall was closed but whose software version cannot be verified due to record gaps. Ford has also noted that other software recalls applied with the legacy IDS tool may have similar problems; as of June 12, 2026, no companion re-recalls have been filed.

Which Ford Focus models and engines are affected?

All 255,404 recalled vehicles are 2012-2018 Ford Focus cars drawn from the 2018 parent recall, which covered 2.0L GDI and 2.0L GTDI (EcoBoost) engines. Ford does not publish a per-model-year breakdown, and the cars were not produced in VIN order — so model year alone never confirms inclusion. Only a per-VIN lookup settles it.

How do I check a specific Focus VIN — and why won't NHTSA show it yet?

Recall 26V369 will not appear in NHTSA's VIN lookup until July 6, 2026, so a "no open recalls" result before that date is not clearance. Until then, the only per-VIN confirmation is Ford's customer line (1-866-436-7332) or a Ford dealer query of the OASIS database. From July 6, enter the VIN at NHTSA.gov/recalls or Ford's owner recall lookup.

For a used-car buyer the practical sequence is:

  1. Pull the VIN from the windshield base or driver's door jamb and run it through NHTSA's free tool (after July 6) or Ford's lookup.
  2. Run a vehicle history check. A Zilocar VIN check screens for recall presence alongside the history that matters more on an aging budget car — accident and damage records, odometer rollback, junk/salvage auction hits, NICB theft records, ownership-chain length, and past sales listings with prices and mileage. NHTSA's tool is free and authoritative for recall status; the history layer is where a paid report earns its fee on a sub-$15K car.
  3. Get written proof of the recall fix from a Ford dealer before money changes hands — a printed OASIS/recall status page, not the seller's word. This recall exists because completion records were wrong once already.

Can a 2012-2018 Focus really stall without warning?

Yes, that is the stated safety risk: an engine stall while driving, without warning, possibly without the ability to restart, which increases crash risk. The mechanism is a canister purge valve in the evaporative emissions system sticking open; during the evaporative leak monitor check, excessive vacuum builds in the fuel system and can deform the plastic fuel tank. Warning signs listed in the filing are the check engine light, an inaccurate fuel gauge, inaccurate distance-to-empty, and drivability concerns.

Context for buyers weighing the risk: the recall carries no "do not drive" or "park outside" advisory, and Ford is aware of no accidents or injuries related to this condition. During the original 2018 campaign, Ford's interim advice was to keep the fuel tank at least half full. The 26V369 remedy is software-only; the original 18S32 remedy went further, replacing the purge valve, carbon canister, fuel tank, and fuel delivery module as needed. Under 26V369, dealers validate the new software via a Software Validation Form before the recall can be closed.

What about the Focus transmission problems — is that a recall too?

No. The DPS6 PowerShift dual-clutch automatic — the source of widespread shudder, slip, and jerk complaints on these cars — was never subject to a US safety recall. It was handled through technical service bulletins, extended warranties, and litigation: the Vargas v. Ford class settlement (court-approved March 5, 2020) covered 2012-2016 Focus and 2011-2016 Fiesta, with separate later claims targeting 2017-2018 Focus DPS6 cars. Manual-transmission cars are unaffected.

This matters for setting expectations: the DPS6 will never show up in any VIN recall lookup, free or paid, because it was never a recall. Transmission risk on an automatic 2012-2018 Focus is screened the old-fashioned way — a pre-purchase inspection with a test drive under load, plus history signals like a long ownership chain or repeated short listings that can indicate an unresolved problem being passed along.

What can a VIN check tell you about this recall — and what can't it?

A VIN check can confirm that recall 26V369 applies to a car (once VINs load on July 6, 2026) and surface the deeper history that dominates risk on an 8-14-year-old budget car — but it cannot confirm the recall was remedied, and this recall is the proof. Ford's own dealer records marked 255,404 repairs "successfully completed" when the software was never correctly installed. No vehicle history report, from any provider, verifies per-unit firmware state.

Frequently asked questions

How do I check if a used Ford Focus is part of recall 26V369? Enter the 17-digit VIN at NHTSA.gov/recalls — but 26V369 VINs only become searchable there on July 6, 2026. Before that date, call Ford at 1-866-436-7332 or ask a Ford dealer to run the VIN through the OASIS database. Model year alone never confirms inclusion because affected cars were not produced in VIN order.

Why is the Ford Focus being recalled again if the 2018 recall was already done? Ford found that some repairs under 2018 recall 18V735/18S32 were recorded as complete even though the PCM software was never correctly installed, or records could not verify it. Recall 26V369 re-opens 255,404 of those 1,282,596 vehicles for a free dealer software update.

What are the warning signs of the Ford Focus stalling defect? Per the NHTSA Part 573 filing: an illuminated check engine light (MIL), an inaccurate fuel gauge, inaccurate distance-to-empty readings, or drivability concerns. The cause is a canister purge valve that can stick open, creating excess vacuum that can deform the plastic fuel tank and stall the engine.

Is the Ford Focus recall repair free? Yes. Dealers update the powertrain control module software free of charge, regardless of who owns the car now. It is not an over-the-air update — the car must go to a dealer. Owners who previously paid out of pocket for related repairs may seek reimbursement under Ford's general plan.

Does a vehicle history report show whether a recall was actually fixed? No. Vehicle history reports from any provider show recall presence, not remedy status. Recall 26V369 exists precisely because Ford's own records marked repairs complete when the software was wrong. Only Ford's VIN lookup, NHTSA's tool (after July 6, 2026), or a dealer OASIS check confirms a specific car's status.

Is the Ford Focus PowerShift transmission covered by a recall? No. The DPS6 PowerShift dual-clutch automatic in 2012-2018 Focus cars was never subject to a US safety recall; complaints were handled through service bulletins, extended warranties, and litigation, including the Vargas v. Ford class settlement covering 2012-2016 Focus. It will never appear in any VIN recall lookup.

Should I still buy a 2012-2018 Ford Focus with this recall open? The recall itself is manageable: the fix is a free dealer software update, the recall carries no do-not-drive or park-outside advisory, and Ford reports no known accidents or injuries. The larger risks on an 8-14-year-old budget car are undisclosed accidents, odometer rollback, salvage-auction history, and transmission condition.

Which Ford Focus engines are affected by recall 26V369? The parent 2018 recall covered 2012-2018 Focus cars with 2.0L GDI and 2.0L GTDI engines; 26V369 is a subset of that population. The 1.0L EcoBoost, Focus Electric, and 2.3L Focus RS were outside the parent recall's engine scope. Only a per-VIN lookup confirms whether a specific car is included.
